## QUESTION 1 — SEQ: Transition of Care [Total: 22 marks] [BOARD]
Time allowed: ~25 minutes

**CLINICAL SCENARIO:**
A 17-year-old male with a history of posterior urethral valves (abated in infancy, valve bladder syndrome managed with CIC) is approaching transition from paediatric to adult urology. He has CKD stage 3b (eGFR 40 mL/min/1.73 m²), uses CIC 4 times daily, and has erectile dysfunction concerns.

**(a)** Outline the components of a structured transition programme from paediatric to adult urology services. **[6 marks]**

**(b)** Discuss the barriers to successful transition in adolescents with congenital urological conditions. **[5 marks]**

**(c)** Explain the pathophysiology of erectile dysfunction in this patient and the management options. **[5 marks]**

**(d)** Discuss his fertility potential and the options available. **[6 marks]**

## QUESTION 2 — SEQ: Sexual Function After Hypospadias [Total: 20 marks] [BOARD]
Time allowed: ~25 minutes

**CLINICAL SCENARIO:**
A 19-year-old man who had TIP repair for distal hypospadias in childhood now presents with concerns about penile curvature during erection, a weak stream, and ejaculatory difficulty. He is sexually active but reports that his partner feels discomfort during intercourse.

**(a)** What are the potential long-term complications of childhood hypospadias repair? **[4 marks]**

**(b)** Discuss the assessment of this patient, including investigations. **[5 marks]**

**(c)** Outline the management options for residual penile curvature (chordee) in an adult. **[5 marks]**

**(d)** Discuss the psychological impact of hypospadias on adult sexual function and body image. **[6 marks]**

## QUESTION 5 — LCV: Transition [Total: 35 marks] [DISTINCTION]
Time allowed: ~40 minutes
*(LCV covering the transition of a patient with spina bifida through adolescence)*

**Q1a.** A 16-year-old with myelomeningocele, CIC-dependent, with known neurogenic bladder. Discuss readiness for transition. [5 marks]
**Q1b.** He has new-onset incontinence despite CIC. What investigations are needed? [5 marks]
**Q2a.** Urodynamics show poor compliance with high pressures. Discuss management. [6 marks]
**Q2b.** He is not interested in talking about sex. How do you approach this? [4 marks]
**Q3a.** At 18, he has CKD stage 4 (eGFR 25). What planning is needed for RRT? [5 marks]
**Q3b.** Discuss renal transplantation in the neurogenic bladder patient. [5 marks]
**Q4a.** He develops recurrent UTIs post-transplant. Discuss management. [2 marks]
**Q4b.** What is the long-term outlook and follow-up? [3 marks]
