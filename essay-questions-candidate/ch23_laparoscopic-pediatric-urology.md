## QUESTION 1 — SEQ: Laparoscopic Nephrectomy [Total: 22 marks] [BOARD]
Time allowed: ~25 minutes

**CLINICAL SCENARIO:**
A 3-year-old girl with a non-functioning left kidney secondary to MCDK, recurrent UTIs and flank pain. You plan a laparoscopic nephrectomy.

**(a)** Compare retroperitoneal and transperitoneal approaches for laparoscopic nephrectomy in a child this age — including positioning, port placement, advantages, and disadvantages. **[6 marks]**

**(b)** Describe the steps of a transperitoneal laparoscopic nephrectomy. **[6 marks]**

**(c)** Discuss the learning curve and conversion rates for laparoscopic nephrectomy in children. **[4 marks]**

**(d)** Compare the outcomes of laparoscopic vs open nephrectomy in children. **[6 marks]**

## QUESTION 6 — EMQ: Laparoscopic Approaches [Total: 10 marks] [BOARD]

**Theme: Choosing a minimally invasive approach**

**Options:** A. Transperitoneal lap. nephrectomy, B. Retroperitoneoscopic nephrectomy, C. Robotic pyeloplasty, D. Lap. Fowler-Stephens, E. Lap. orchidopexy, F. Lap. ureteric reimplantation, G. Open approach, H. Retroperitoneoscopic heminephrectomy, I. Lap. pyelolithotomy/PCNL, J. Cystoscopic pneumovesicoscopic reimplantation

**Scenarios:**
1. 6-month-old with MCDK needing nephrectomy. [B]
2. 4-year-old with PUJO needing pyeloplasty. [C or A]
3. 9-month-old with bilateral impalpable testes. [D/E]
4. 3-year-old with VUR needing reimplant — consider [J] 
5. 2-year-old with symptomatic UPJO in solitary kidney. [C/G]
