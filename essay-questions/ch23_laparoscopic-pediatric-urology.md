## QUESTION 1 — SEQ: Laparoscopic Nephrectomy [Total: 22 marks] [BOARD]
Time allowed: ~25 minutes

**CLINICAL SCENARIO:**
A 3-year-old girl with a non-functioning left kidney secondary to MCDK, recurrent UTIs and flank pain. You plan a laparoscopic nephrectomy.

**(a)** Compare retroperitoneal and transperitoneal approaches for laparoscopic nephrectomy in a child this age — including positioning, port placement, advantages, and disadvantages. **[6 marks]**

**(b)** Describe the steps of a transperitoneal laparoscopic nephrectomy. **[6 marks]**

**(c)** Discuss the learning curve and conversion rates for laparoscopic nephrectomy in children. **[4 marks]**

**(d)** Compare the outcomes of laparoscopic vs open nephrectomy in children. **[6 marks]**

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**EXAMINER'S MARKING GUIDE**

**(a)** [6 marks] — 1 per valid point up to 6:
• Retroperitoneal: positioning lateral decubitus, access via open (Hasson) technique, balloon dilation creates workspace [1]
   • Advantages: direct access to kidney, no bowel manipulation [1]
   • Disadvantages: smaller workspace, higher CO₂ absorption, steeper learning curve [1]
• Transperitoneal: lateral decubitus ± slight supine, camera at umbilicus, working ports in flank [1]
   • Advantages: larger workspace, familiar anatomy, easier for larger specimens [1]
   • Disadvantages: need to mobilise colon, risk of bowel injury, potential ileus [1]

**(b)** [6 marks]
• Position: lateral decubitus with slight table flexion [1]
• Insufflation: Veress needle or open (Hasson) entry [1]
• Ports: 5 mm camera at umbilicus, two 3-5 mm working ports in ipsilateral flank [1]
• Mobilise colon medially (white line of Toldt) [1]
• Identify ureter — dissect and divide between clips [1]
• Identify and ligate renal vessels — seal or clip artery first, then vein [1]
• Mobilise kidney and deliver through enlarged port site or morcellate [1]

**(c)** [4 marks]
• Learning curve: ~20-30 cases to achieve proficiency [1]
• Conversion rates: ~2-5% in experienced hands; higher in early series [1]
• Causes: bleeding, adhesions, difficult anatomy, equipment failure [1]
• Conversion to open is NOT a complication — it is a sound judgment [1]

**(d)** [6 marks]
• Laparoscopic: longer operative time (60-120 min), shorter hospital stay (24-48h), less analgesia [1]
• Open: shorter OR time, longer stay (3-5 days), more post-op pain [1]
• Laparoscopic: better cosmesis, quicker return to normal activity [1]
• Both: equivalent complication rates (~5-10%) [1]
• Laparoscopic: equivalent outcomes for non-functioning kidneys [1]
• Laparoscopic preferred unless contraindicated (severe adhesions, coagulopathy, large specimen) [1]

## QUESTION 2 — SEQ: Robotic Pyeloplasty [Total: 20 marks] [DISTINCTION]
Time allowed: ~25 minutes

**CLINICAL SCENARIO:**
A 5-year-old with right PUJ obstruction (symptomatic, 38% DRF) undergoes robotic pyeloplasty.

**(a)** Describe the docking and port placement for robotic pyeloplasty in a 5-year-old. **[5 marks]**

**(b)** Compare the outcomes of robotic pyeloplasty with open pyeloplasty in children, including operative time, success rate, and cost. **[6 marks]**

**(c)** Discuss the specific challenges of robotic surgery in infants (<1 year old). **[5 marks]**

**(d)** Outline the future of robotic paediatric urology. **[4 marks]**

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**EXAMINER'S MARKING GUIDE**

**(a)** [5 marks]
• Position: lateral decubitus at 45-60° [1]
• Camera port: umbilicus (12 mm) [1]
• Two working ports: 5-8 mm in ipsilateral flank (subcostal and iliac) [1]
• Assistant port: 5 mm between camera and working ports [1]
• Docking: robot comes from behind (cephalad orientation to the kidney) [1]

**(b)** [6 marks]
• Operative time: robotic longer than open (130-180 vs 90-120 min) [1]
• Success: comparable — >95% for both [1]
• Hospital stay: shorter for robotic (24-48h vs 72-96h open) [1]
• Analgesia use: less in robotic [1]
• Cosmesis: better with robotic [1]
• Cost: robotic significantly more expensive (robot purchase/maintenance + disposables) [1]

**(c)** [5 marks]
• Smaller working space — port placement more critical [1]
• Smaller abdominal cavity — instruments may clash externally [1]
• Thinner abdominal wall — risk of gas leak, need for stay sutures at ports [1]
• Anesthetic considerations: longer pneumoperitoneum time, CO₂ absorption [1]
• Alternative: open pyeloplasty is safe and effective with smaller incisions in infants [1]

**(d)** [4 marks]
• Smaller robots designed for children (size reduction, flexible instruments) [1]
• Augmented reality integration: intra-operative navigation [1]
• Reduced costs with competition and increased adoption [1]
• Tele-mentoring and remote surgery possibilities [1]

## QUESTION 3 — SAQ: Laparoscopic Orchidopexy [Total: 8 marks] [FOUNDATION]
Time allowed: ~10 minutes

**(a)** Describe the steps of diagnostic laparoscopy for the impalpable testis. **[4 marks]**

**(b)** Outline the laparoscopic one-stage Fowler-Stephens orchidopexy. **[4 marks]**

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**MARKING GUIDE:**

**(a)** [4 marks]
• 5 mm umbilical camera port [1]
• Insufflate to 10-12 mmHg [1]
• Inspect internal ring: observe spermatic vessels entering (testis present) or blind-ending (vanishing) [1]
• Examine pelvis for intra-abdominal testis at the tip of the gubernaculum [1]

**(b)** [4 marks]
• Mobilise testis, divide gubernaculum [1]
• Ligate and divide testicular vessels between clips (at level of internal ring) [1]
• Create medial peritoneal window for tension-free passage [1]
• Bring testis through medial canal into scrotum [1]

## QUESTION 4 — SAQ: Complications [Total: 8 marks] [BOARD]
Time allowed: ~10 minutes

**(a)** List four specific complications of laparoscopic surgery in children. **[4 marks]**

**(b)** State how each can be prevented or managed. **[4 marks]**

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
**MARKING GUIDE:**

**(a) & (b)** [4 marks]
• CO₂ embolism — prevent by careful insufflation; treat: release pneumo, left lateral Trendelenburg [1]
• Port-site hernia (5 mm+ sites) — close fascia for >5 mm ports [1]
• Bowel injury (entry-related) — open (Hasson) technique preferred in children with prior surgery [1]
• Hypothermia — warm irrigation, forced air warmer, low-flow anaesthesia [1]
